Why treating yourself
with humour can be good for you?

  • One way you can cope with sudden unexpected tension or indeed periods of heightened tension is for you to, simply adopt a less serious attitude to life yourself – “LIGHTEN UP”! – relax, breathe and smile, take others less seriously
  • Smiling and laughing release ENDORPHINS, our system’s natural opiates which research shows decrease anger and anxiety levels, as well as relieving pain and boosting your immune system. Sometimes referred to as “internal jogging”.
  • Some research suggests humour relieves/reduces the physiological arousal that is associated with fear and apprehension.
  • Other research shows that humour can “cushion” the negative impact of situations that were previously linked with anxiety and fear by injecting a positive set of emotions.
  • The positive effects of humour may also be partly attributable to the emotional distancing that follows laughter. The feeling associated with laughter is incompatible with the physiological states connected with anxiety, fear, depression and anger.
  • Some evidence that during and immediately following spouts of spontaneous laughter, muscle tension begins to lessen.
